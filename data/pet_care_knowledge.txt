Dogs need regular exercise, fresh water, balanced meals, and routine veterinary checkups. Puppies usually need more frequent meals than adult dogs. 
Cats need clean litter boxes, fresh water, balanced food, scratching surfaces, and routine veterinary care. Sudden changes in appetite, hiding, or litter box habits may be signs of stress or illness.
Chocolate, grapes, raisins, onions, garlic, xylitol, and certain human medications can be dangerous or toxic to pets. If a pet eats a toxic food or chemical, the owner should contact a veterinarian or pet poison hotline immediately.
Vomiting, seizures, trouble breathing, collapse, bleeding, or signs of severe pain may be emergencies. A pet owner should contact a veterinarian or emergency clinic immediately.
Pet matching should consider lifestyle, home size, allergies, schedule, budget, and activity level. Busy students may do better with lower-maintenance pets or adult animals instead of high-energy puppies.
AI pet-care tools should not replace veterinarians. They can provide general guidance, but medical diagnosis and treatment decisions should come from licensed professionals.
